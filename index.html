Welcome!

I am a computational biologist specializing in machine learning applications in genomics and drug discovery. My research focuses on developing novel algorithms to analyze large-scale biological datasets and identify therapeutic targets for complex diseases.

I am currently a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the University Institute of Advanced Studies, working in the Computational Systems Biology Lab under Dr. Principal Investigator.

Research Interests:

Machine Learning Genomics Drug Discovery Systems Biology Bioinformatics Deep Learning

Current Research

Project 1: Deep Learning for Protein Structure Prediction

Developing novel neural network architectures to predict protein folding patterns and protein-protein interactions from sequence data. This work has implications for understanding disease mechanisms and designing new therapeutics.

Project 2: Multi-Omics Data Integration

Creating computational frameworks to integrate genomics, transcriptomics, and proteomics data for comprehensive understanding of cancer biology. Our approach uses graph neural networks to model complex molecular interactions.

Project 3: Drug Repurposing Pipeline

Building automated pipelines for identifying existing drugs that could be repurposed for new therapeutic applications using network pharmacology and machine learning approaches.
